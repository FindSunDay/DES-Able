Disability discrimination

Disability discrimination happens at workplace when a person with disability is treated less favourably, or not given the same opportunities as others in a similar situation.

more
Law and rights

There are many laws can gives you  protection from any such discrimination. And it is important that you understand your rights at your workplace.
 

more
make a complaint

If you are being discriminated at workplace and you cannot deal with the situation yourself, you can make a complaint to the Australian Human Rights Commission.

When you apply for a job, they reject you because of your disability even you have better CV.

Being ignored in positation promotation at workplace because of your disability, even you work hard and have better performance than others.

How To Make A Complaint

Make a complaint to the Australian Human Right Commission

01

Fill the complaint form the commission, and you can choose any language you like.

02

Provide sufficient details about the allegation on covering what happened, when and where as well as who was involved.

03

You have the provision to get support form the commission to write the complaint.

04

Send the form as a post or fax to the commission's office.

05

You can lodge a complaint online from the commission's website or send a mail to the commission specifying the incident.

What Happens After The Complaint

01

You may be contacted by the commissionstaff once the complaint is received.

02

The accused person or organisation will be informed about the complaint and be given a copy of the same.

03

As the commission collects detailed information regarding the incident, you will be invited to participate in a conciliation.

04

You and the accused will be allowed to talk about the issue.

05

The commission will try to come up with a solution to resolve the complaint.
